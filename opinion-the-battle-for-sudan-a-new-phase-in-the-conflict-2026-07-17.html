The Battle for Sudan: A New Phase in the Conflict

OpinionSudanSouth SudanEritrea By Amira Hassan  ·  July 17, 2026
Opinion notice: This is analysis and commentary by Horn Updates editors. It does not represent the position of any government, institution, or external party.
What this means
  • Sudan's military gains could reshape power dynamics
  • Humanitarian crisis worsening amid ongoing conflict
  • Regional actors recalibrating their strategies in response

The ongoing conflict in Sudan has entered a new and critical phase as the Sudanese Armed Forces (SAF) report substantial victories against the Rapid Support Forces (RSF). This week, the SAF announced the destruction of 205 RSF combat vehicles and the downing of four drones. These developments, while indicative of military progress for the regular army, raise significant concerns about the humanitarian fallout and the broader implications for regional stability.

Military Progress and Its Implications

The SAF's recent claims highlight a turning point in the conflict that erupted in April 2023. The fighting has been characterized by intense urban warfare and a brutal struggle for control over key territories in Sudan. The destruction of RSF assets underscores the capacity of the SAF to mount a significant offensive, which could alter the balance of power within Sudan. However, military victories often come at a steep humanitarian cost, as civilians continue to bear the brunt of the conflict.

Reports indicate that the humanitarian crisis in Sudan is worsening, with millions displaced and facing acute food shortages. The UN has warned that nearly 25 million people—more than half of Sudan's population—require humanitarian assistance. The SAF's military successes may embolden its leadership but risk further entrenching the RSF in a protracted conflict where both sides are unwilling to negotiate. With the international community's focus on military developments, the plight of civilians risks being overshadowed.

Regional Repercussions and Geopolitical Dynamics

The implications of the SAF's military advances extend beyond Sudan's borders. As the conflict escalates, neighboring countries such as South Sudan and Eritrea are recalibrating their responses. South Sudan, which has its own complex issues with governance and conflict, is closely monitoring the situation. The Abyei region, a disputed area between Sudan and South Sudan, has seen protests from residents barred from participating in South Sudan's elections due to ongoing instability. This exclusion highlights the fragility of the peace process and raises fears of renewed tensions along the border.

Eritrea, a nation with historical ties to Sudanese conflicts, is also affected by the shifting dynamics. As Eritrea grapples with its own internal issues, the potential influx of refugees from Sudan poses a significant challenge. Eritrean authorities may feel compelled to intervene in some capacity, further complicating an already intricate regional landscape. The potential for a spillover effect, with conflicts in Sudan fuelling unrest in neighboring countries, cannot be underestimated.

International Engagement and Humanitarian Concerns

International responses to the conflict have been varied, with some countries expressing support for the SAF, while others have called for a ceasefire and dialogue. The United States, for example, has condemned the violence and urged both sides to engage in peace talks. However, the efficacy of such diplomatic efforts remains uncertain, especially as military engagements intensify.

Moreover, the humanitarian crisis is drawing the attention of international organizations, with calls for increased aid and support for those affected. The United Nations and various NGOs are struggling to operate amid the chaos, often finding their access severely restricted. The SAF's military actions may hinder humanitarian operations, exacerbating the already dire conditions for civilians caught in the crossfire.

As military operations continue, the international community faces a critical juncture. The risk of prolonged conflict looms large, and without a concerted effort to address the humanitarian needs of the Sudanese people, the cycle of violence may persist. The challenge lies not only in achieving military objectives but also in fostering an environment conducive to peace and stability.

Looking Ahead: Strategic Considerations for the Horn of Africa

In assessing the situation in Sudan, it is vital to consider the strategic implications for the Horn of Africa. The military victories of the SAF could embolden other regional actors, prompting a reevaluation of alliances and power structures. As the conflict unfolds, countries in the region must navigate a complex landscape where military strength and humanitarian considerations are often at odds.

Looking ahead, a critical observation is that the resolution of the Sudanese conflict will require a multifaceted approach that prioritizes not only military solutions but also robust humanitarian support and diplomatic engagement. The international community must engage strategically, recognizing that the stability of Sudan is intrinsically linked to the broader security and humanitarian landscape of the Horn of Africa. As the situation evolves, a failure to address these interconnected issues could lead to further instability, with far-reaching consequences for the region.
