The Role of AI in Sudan's Ongoing Conflict: A Double-Edged Sword

OpinionSudanSouth Sudan By Amira Hassan  ·  May 13, 2026
Opinion notice: This is analysis and commentary by Horn Updates editors. It does not represent the position of any government, institution, or external party.
What this means
  • AI could enhance security operations in Sudan
  • Human rights abuses may increase
  • Future conflicts could be tech-driven

The ongoing conflict in Sudan has reached alarming levels, marked by a humanitarian crisis and a political vacuum. As the country endures its 1,115th day of war, a new player is emerging on the battlefield: artificial intelligence (AI). While AI holds the potential to transform military operations and governance, its implications for human rights and civilian safety are deeply concerning. In a nation where governance and ethical frameworks are already fragile, the integration of AI into military and security operations raises critical questions about accountability, oversight, and the future trajectory of this conflict.

AI's Potential in Military Operations

AI technologies have been increasingly adopted by governments and military organizations worldwide, promising enhanced decision-making capabilities, operational efficiency, and real-time data analysis. For Sudan's security forces, which have been struggling to maintain control amidst civil unrest, AI could serve as a tool for improved surveillance and intelligence gathering. This is especially relevant given the fragmented nature of the armed groups involved in the conflict, such as the Sudan Armed Forces (SAF) and the Rapid Support Forces (RSF). By employing AI for predictive analytics, the military could potentially pre-empt attacks, optimize resource allocation, and streamline logistics.

On June 1, 2023, the U.S. government highlighted the importance of technology in modern warfare, noting that nations like Russia and China are already investing heavily in AI for military applications. Sudan, facing an urgent need to modernize its military tactics, may feel pressure to follow suit. However, the rapid adoption of AI technology without robust ethical guidelines and oversight could lead to unintended consequences.

Escalating Human Rights Violations

While the operational advantages offered by AI may be appealing, they come with the potential for grave human rights violations. The Sudanese military has a troubling history of abuses, including indiscriminate attacks on civilians, arbitrary detentions, and torture. The introduction of AI into these already problematic dynamics raises significant concerns. Automated surveillance systems could be used to monitor dissent, while AI-driven data analysis might enable targeted actions against specific populations deemed 'threatening' to the regime.

Moreover, the lack of transparency surrounding AI algorithms means that accountability for these actions is murky at best. The potential for AI to facilitate mass surveillance or even autonomous weapon systems without adequate checks is a chilling prospect. As the conflict continues, the risk of escalating violence against civilians becomes ever more pronounced, particularly in areas already suffering from humanitarian crises.

The Regional and Global Implications

The implications of AI's integration into Sudan's conflict extend beyond its borders, affecting regional stability and international relations. Neighboring countries like South Sudan, which has its own history of conflict and instability, could see spillover effects as AI technologies alter the dynamics of warfare in the region. The heightened surveillance and potential for human rights abuses could prompt a refugee crisis, further straining resources in an already vulnerable area.

Furthermore, the international community's response will be crucial. Recent discussions around U.S. visa restrictions and calls for sanctions against Sudanese officials highlight the growing recognition of the need for a unified international stance on human rights. However, the challenge remains: how can external actors influence Sudan's military strategies while respecting the complexities of its internal politics? The risk is that without a coherent diplomatic approach, foreign intervention could inadvertently escalate conflicts, making the situation even more volatile.

A Call for Responsible AI Governance

As Sudan navigates this precarious landscape, the need for responsible AI governance cannot be overstated. Stakeholders, including international organizations and technology firms, must come together to establish ethical frameworks and guidelines that govern the use of AI in conflict zones. These frameworks should prioritize human rights and civilian safety, ensuring that technological advancements do not come at the expense of humanitarian principles.

Moreover, the international community must remain vigilant in monitoring how AI is deployed in Sudan. This includes advocating for transparency in military operations and holding actors accountable for violations stemming from AI misuse. The ongoing humanitarian crisis, exacerbated by the conflict, makes it imperative that any technological advancements serve to protect, rather than endanger, civilian lives.

As Sudan continues to grapple with its multi-faceted crisis, the introduction of AI into military operations could either pave the way for a more strategic resolution or further entrench the cycle of violence. The path forward lies in balancing the potential benefits of technology with the urgent need for ethical governance and accountability. Only then can Sudan hope to emerge from its protracted conflict with a semblance of stability and a commitment to human rights.

Looking ahead, the challenge will not only be to monitor the use of AI in Sudan but also to advocate for a broader dialogue around its implications for conflict resolution and human rights. As AI continues to evolve, so too must our approaches to governance and accountability in the complex landscape of modern warfare.
