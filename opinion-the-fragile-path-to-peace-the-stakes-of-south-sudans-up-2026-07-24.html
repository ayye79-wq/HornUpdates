The Fragile Path to Peace: The Stakes of South Sudan's Upcoming Elections

OpinionSouth Sudan By Amira Hassan  ·  July 24, 2026
Opinion notice: This is analysis and commentary by Horn Updates editors. It does not represent the position of any government, institution, or external party.
What this means
  • South Sudan: elections could ignite renewed conflict
  • Domestic pressures may undermine peace process
  • International scrutiny intensifying ahead of elections

As South Sudan approaches its long-awaited elections in December 2024, the stakes could not be higher. The fragile peace established through the Revitalized Agreement on the Resolution of the Conflict in South Sudan (R-ARCSS) is increasingly threatened by political maneuvering and deep-rooted societal tensions. Recent headlines from our newsroom underscore the urgency of this situation, revealing not only the ongoing humanitarian challenges but also the implications of political decisions that could either safeguard or shatter the nation’s tenuous stability.

The Political Landscape: A Precarious Balance

The political landscape in South Sudan is fraught with complexities that could derail the peace process. The transitional government, formed in February 2020, is tasked with steering the country towards elections, yet the lack of a comprehensive electoral framework raises concerns about legitimacy and inclusivity. As observed in the op-ed "South Sudan needs peace, not sham elections," the current political climate suggests that the elections may devolve into a mere formality rather than a genuine exercise in democracy.

Notably, the recent revocation of three bomas (administrative units) by the Bor commissioner to defuse boundary disputes reflects the local tensions that are likely to exacerbate as elections approach. Such administrative maneuvers might temporarily calm disputes, but they underscore the fragility of local governance and the potential for conflict if not managed effectively. The fear is that unresolved grievances could lead to violence, especially in areas with a history of ethnic strife.

International Influence: A Double-Edged Sword

The role of international actors in South Sudan's upcoming elections cannot be overstated. The U.S. has long been a key player in the region, and the recent call for restoring U.S. leverage highlights a growing recognition of the need for proactive diplomatic engagement. However, mere symbolic diplomacy is insufficient; tangible measures must accompany rhetoric to foster genuine political reform.

China's pledge of stronger military cooperation with South Sudan adds another layer of complexity to the international landscape. While this may provide the South Sudanese government with the security apparatus it seeks, it also raises questions about the implications for human rights and governance. The partnership could embolden the government at a time when accountability and transparency are desperately needed. Such dynamics may complicate the environment for free and fair elections, particularly if the military is used to suppress dissent in the lead-up to the polls.

The Human Cost: A Focus on Vulnerable Populations

The humanitarian crisis continues to unfold, revealing a dire need for comprehensive support for the most vulnerable populations. Recent distributions of dignity kits to vulnerable girls and young mothers in Luri Payam exemplify the ongoing struggle for basic human rights and dignity amidst political turmoil. These initiatives are critical, yet they operate in a vacuum of broader governance failures and societal instability.

The European Union’s envoy’s pointed questions to South Sudanese MPs regarding the minuscule spending on education and health speak volumes about the governance challenges that persist. Without addressing these foundational issues, the prospect of conducting credible elections looms ever larger. The risk is that without significant investment in human capital, the governance structures will remain weak, rendering the electoral process ineffective and potentially violent.

Looking Ahead: The Imperative for Genuine Engagement

As South Sudan navigates the treacherous waters leading up to the 2024 elections, the imperative for genuine engagement from both domestic stakeholders and international partners is clearer than ever. The recent power deal signed in Northern Bahr el Ghazal to bring electricity to Aweil town is a promising development that could serve as a model for future infrastructure projects. Yet, without a commitment to inclusive governance and a focus on the rule of law, such initiatives risk being overshadowed by political maneuvering and unrest.

In conclusion, the path ahead for South Sudan is precarious. For the elections to serve as a step towards lasting peace rather than a catalyst for renewed conflict, all stakeholders must prioritize transparency, inclusivity, and accountability. The upcoming months are critical, and the actions taken now will determine whether South Sudan can embark on a more stable and prosperous future or continue to be mired in cycles of violence and instability.
