Sudan's RSF: The Veiled Threat of Impunity in El Fasher

OpinionSudan By Amira Hassan  ·  July 1, 2026
Opinion notice: This is analysis and commentary by Horn Updates editors. It does not represent the position of any government, institution, or external party.
What this means
  • Sudan: RSF faces international scrutiny
  • Humanitarian crisis deepening in Darfur
  • Impunity risks escalating violence further

The ongoing conflict in Sudan, particularly the situation involving the Rapid Support Forces (RSF), continues to draw international attention due to its alarming implications for human rights and regional stability. Recent revelations from Amnesty International regarding the RSF's alleged crimes against humanity in El Fasher serve as a stark reminder of the deteriorating conditions in the country. As violence escalates, the question of accountability becomes more pressing, revealing a troubling trend of impunity that poses severe risks not only to Sudan but to the broader Horn of Africa region.

Amnesty's Accusations: A Window into Atrocities

On October 9, 2023, Amnesty International published a report detailing the RSF's involvement in systematic human rights violations in El Fasher, the capital of North Darfur. The organization alleges that the RSF has committed acts amounting to crimes against humanity, including extrajudicial killings, enforced disappearances, and widespread sexual violence. This is not the first time the RSF, originally formed from Janjaweed militias, has been implicated in such heinous acts; their violent legacy stretches back to the Darfur conflict that erupted in the early 2000s. The current report, however, underscores a disturbing escalation in the scale and brutality of their actions.

The Amnesty report is based on extensive interviews with victims and witnesses, revealing a chilling pattern of abuse that suggests a calculated strategy of terror employed by the RSF to consolidate power amid the chaos of the ongoing civil conflict. As the Sudanese military and RSF compete for control, the civilian population bears the brunt of their struggle, leading to an increasingly dire humanitarian crisis. The implications of these findings are profound, highlighting not only the immediate threats faced by civilians but also the broader challenges of ensuring accountability in a context where state structures have largely collapsed.

Regional Implications: The Broader Fallout of Impunity

The situation in Sudan has significant ramifications for the stability of the Horn of Africa. As the RSF operates with apparent impunity, it sets a dangerous precedent for other armed groups in the region. The lack of accountability for these violations emboldens similar factions across neighboring countries, where power struggles and ethnic tensions are often exacerbated by external influences and internal grievances. The spillover effects of violence and instability are already being felt in Chad, South Sudan, and the Central African Republic, where armed groups exploit the chaos for their own agendas.

Moreover, the humanitarian crisis that has ensued from the conflict is reaching critical levels. The United Nations has reported that over 5 million people are displaced within Sudan, while millions more are in urgent need of humanitarian assistance. As the RSF continues to perpetrate violence, the international community faces a critical juncture: will it take decisive action to hold perpetrators accountable, or will it allow the cycle of impunity to perpetuate, further jeopardizing the lives of vulnerable populations?

The Role of the International Community: A Call for Action

The international community has been largely reactive in addressing the crises in Sudan. While organizations like Amnesty International have raised awareness of the atrocities committed by the RSF, direct intervention or pressure for accountability has been lacking. The United Nations Mission in South Sudan (UNMISS) has recently closed a temporary operating base in Akobo, signaling a potential drawdown of international presence in the region. This move raises concerns about the diminishing support for peacekeeping efforts amid escalating violence in neighboring states.

Furthermore, the lack of effective sanctions or consequences for the RSF sends a troubling signal to both perpetrators and victims. Without a robust international response, including targeted sanctions against RSF leaders and support for transitional justice mechanisms, the cycle of violence is likely to continue. The failure to address these issues not only undermines the prospects for peace in Sudan but also threatens to escalate tensions across the region.

Looking Ahead: The Need for a Unified Response

As Sudan stands at a crossroads, the imperative for a coherent and unified international response has never been more critical. The allegations against the RSF must be met with urgent calls for accountability, including potential sanctions and a reevaluation of international partnerships with Sudanese military leaders. Simultaneously, humanitarian assistance must be scaled up to address the pressing needs of displaced populations.

Ultimately, the future of Sudan hinges on addressing the impunity that has long plagued its political landscape. As the RSF consolidates its power through violence and intimidation, the risk of further destabilization looms large. The international community must not only respond to the immediate humanitarian crisis but also lay the groundwork for a lasting peace that includes mechanisms for accountability and justice. Without such measures, the cycle of violence is destined to repeat, engulfing both Sudan and its neighbors in an ever-deepening crisis.
