The Hidden Cost of Gum Arabic: Sudan’s War Economy and Its Global Implications

OpinionSudanSouth SudanSomalia By Amira Hassan  ·  July 16, 2026
Opinion notice: This is analysis and commentary by Horn Updates editors. It does not represent the position of any government, institution, or external party.
What this means
  • Sudan's gum arabic trade under scrutiny
  • International markets face supply disruptions
  • Conflict economy exacerbates humanitarian crisis

In the midst of Sudan’s escalating conflict, a seemingly innocuous commodity—gum arabic—has emerged as a focal point of the war economy, revealing the complexities of a nation at war. As the fighting continues, the implications of this natural resource extend beyond Sudan's borders, impacting global industries and humanitarian efforts. Understanding the role of gum arabic in Sudan’s economy is essential to grasping the larger narrative of the ongoing crisis and its far-reaching effects.

The Role of Gum Arabic in Sudan’s Economy

Gum arabic, derived from the acacia tree, is a vital export for Sudan, accounting for over 80% of the world’s supply. This natural gum is used in various industries, including food production, pharmaceuticals, and cosmetics. In 2021, Sudan exported approximately 30,000 tons of gum arabic, generating around $150 million in revenue, which significantly supports the national economy. However, the ongoing conflict has disrupted this lucrative trade, leading to a collapse of local markets and livelihoods in regions heavily reliant on gum production.

The conflict has not only affected production but has also altered control dynamics within the gum arabic supply chain. Armed groups have increasingly taken over the production zones, extorting local farmers and traders, which has driven many to abandon their farms. As a result, the cost of gum arabic has surged, putting a strain on industries that depend on this essential ingredient. The United Nations has reported that prices have increased by over 50% in some regions, leading to inflation in related sectors. This highlights the intertwining of local economies with the broader landscape of conflict, where violence directly translates into economic hardship for civilians.

International Implications and Market Vulnerabilities

The ramifications of Sudan’s gum arabic trade extend far beyond its borders. Global industries are now facing the prospect of supply chain disruptions as the conflict escalates. Notably, the food and beverage sector, which relies heavily on gum arabic as a stabilizer and emulsifier, is becoming increasingly vulnerable. Companies such as Coca-Cola and Nestlé are at risk of facing shortages, leading to potential increases in product prices or altering formulations that may affect quality.

Moreover, the volatility in gum arabic supply could push companies to seek alternative sources, potentially leading to increased deforestation and environmental degradation in other regions where gum-producing acacia trees grow. This shift could have long-lasting ecological consequences, as companies may inadvertently contribute to the destruction of vital ecosystems in their quest for sustainability and supply chain security.

The Humanitarian Crisis: A War Economy's Toll on Civilians

The humanitarian impact of the gum arabic trade in the context of Sudan’s war economy is profound. As armed factions vie for control over production areas, civilians are increasingly caught in the crossfire. The UN estimates that more than 20 million people in Sudan are in need of humanitarian assistance, with access to food and basic services severely compromised by the conflict.

Farmers, who once relied on gum arabic as a steady income source, now face violence and displacement, forcing them to abandon their agricultural practices. This not only undermines local economies but also exacerbates food insecurity, as many farmers also grew crops for subsistence. The World Food Programme has highlighted that the disruption of agricultural practices will lead to a long-term decline in food production, creating a vicious cycle of poverty and dependence on international aid.

Looking Ahead: Navigating the Challenges of Conflict and Commerce

As the situation in Sudan deteriorates, the international community must grapple with the complexities of humanitarian aid and conflict-driven economies. The gum arabic trade serves as a critical case study for understanding how local resources can become entangled in national crises. Stakeholders must consider not only immediate humanitarian support but also longer-term strategies for stabilizing the economy and rebuilding institutions.

One potential avenue is to promote fair trade practices that ensure farmers receive adequate compensation for their products, even in times of conflict. Initiatives that empower local communities to engage in ethical production methods could help mitigate the impact of armed groups on the gum arabic trade. Furthermore, international consumers and companies must be aware of the origins of their products and advocate for transparency in supply chains to avoid complicity in ongoing violence.

Ultimately, the fate of gum arabic—and by extension, the livelihoods of countless Sudanese—hinges on a delicate balance between conflict resolution and economic recovery. The international community must remain engaged and proactive in addressing the humanitarian crisis while supporting sustainable practices that allow local economies to flourish amidst adversity.
