The Unfolding Humanitarian Crisis in Sudan: A Call to Action

OpinionSudan By Amira Hassan  ·  June 16, 2026
Opinion notice: This is analysis and commentary by Horn Updates editors. It does not represent the position of any government, institution, or external party.
What this means
  • Humanitarian conditions deteriorating rapidly
  • International response remains inadequate
  • Civilian casualties escalating amid conflict

The conflict in Sudan, which has intensified since April 2023, has devolved into a humanitarian crisis that demands immediate attention from the international community. The recent drone attacks, which have escalated in frequency and lethality, are a stark reminder of the urgent need for a coordinated response to the suffering inflicted upon civilians. As the death toll rises, so does the responsibility of global actors to intervene before the situation deteriorates further.

Drone Warfare: A New Dimension of Destruction

The United Nations reported a disturbing increase in civilian casualties due to drone strikes, with the death toll climbing into the thousands. These attacks, primarily targeting urban centers and civilian infrastructure, reflect a troubling shift in warfare tactics employed by both the Sudanese Armed Forces (SAF) and the Rapid Support Forces (RSF). According to a UN report issued on June 5, 2023, drone strikes have accounted for over 60% of the casualties reported in Khartoum and other regions, including Darfur, where communities are already vulnerable due to years of conflict.

Drone warfare, often characterized by its precision, has resulted in indiscriminate harm to civilians, raising serious ethical questions about its use in populated areas. The use of such technology not only complicates military engagements but also highlights the stark reality: civilians are increasingly caught in the crossfire of a power struggle between entrenched military leaders. This trend suggests that the conflict is likely to deepen, with more civilians facing the brunt of violence if international players do not act swiftly.

Humanitarian Access: A Grave Challenge

The escalating violence has severely hampered humanitarian access in Sudan. According to the UN Office for the Coordination of Humanitarian Affairs (OCHA), approximately 24 million people—over half the population—are in urgent need of assistance. Yet, aid organizations face relentless obstacles, including targeted attacks on aid convoys and bureaucratic barriers imposed by both the SAF and RSF. As of June 2023, only 30% of the required humanitarian funding has been met, leaving organizations unable to provide critical support to displaced populations.

Access to basic necessities such as food, water, and medical supplies is dwindling. For instance, a recent report highlighted that food security levels have plummeted, with 18 million people projected to face acute hunger by September 2023. The situation is exacerbated by rising inflation and a collapsing economy, further straining the resources of ordinary Sudanese citizens.

The international community must prioritize the facilitation of humanitarian corridors and press both conflicting parties to respect international law, which mandates the protection of civilians and the unhindered access to humanitarian aid. Without such measures, the humanitarian landscape in Sudan will continue to deteriorate, leading to greater loss of life and suffering.

The Role of the International Community

The international response to the crisis in Sudan has been lackluster, characterized by a mix of condemnation and limited sanctions. While the UN Security Council has called for a ceasefire and a return to dialogue, the reality on the ground suggests that these calls have had little impact. The entrenched interests of military leaders, coupled with regional geopolitical dynamics, complicate any potential for resolution.

For instance, Egypt and Saudi Arabia have significant stakes in the region and may influence the dynamics of the conflict, yet their involvement has not translated into effective peacemaking efforts. Instead, the focus seems to remain on maintaining the status quo rather than addressing the root causes of the conflict. Furthermore, regional organizations such as the African Union have yet to take decisive steps to mediate effectively between the conflicting parties.

It is imperative for the international community to adopt a multi-faceted approach that combines diplomatic pressure with actionable support for humanitarian efforts. This includes not only financial assistance but also logistical support for NGOs working on the ground. Engaging with regional players and fostering a dialogue that includes civil society voices will be essential for any meaningful resolution.

Looking Ahead: The Path to Stabilization

As the situation in Sudan continues to evolve, it is clear that the humanitarian crisis is set to worsen unless immediate and decisive action is taken. The international community must galvanize a collective response that prioritizes the protection of civilians and the provision of humanitarian aid, while also seeking to hold the military leaders accountable for their actions.

In light of the ongoing violence and the apparent disregard for civilian life, it is crucial to envision a future where a sustainable peace process is established. This will require not only the cessation of hostilities but also the implementation of political reforms that address the grievances of marginalized communities. With elections on the horizon, the risk of deepening instability looms large if these reforms are not prioritized. The time for action is now; without it, the prospects for peace in Sudan will remain bleak.
