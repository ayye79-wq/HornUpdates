The Iranian Influence in Sudan: A Growing Concern for Regional Stability

OpinionSudanEritreaSomaliaDjibouti By Amira Hassan  ·  April 21, 2026
Opinion notice: This is analysis and commentary by Horn Updates editors. It does not represent the position of any government, institution, or external party.

The situation in Sudan has spiraled into unprecedented chaos since the onset of conflict between rival military factions in April 2023. As the humanitarian crisis deepens, another pressing concern has emerged: the burgeoning Iranian influence in Port Sudan. Recent headlines highlight Iranian military activities and the alleged involvement of individuals like Shimeem Mafi, accused of facilitating arms deals for Sudan's military and the Muslim Brotherhood. This development not only poses a threat to Sudan's already fragile security environment but also has far-reaching implications for the entire Horn of Africa.

The Iranian Military Footprint in Port Sudan

Reports suggest that Iranian military personnel, operating under the guise of humanitarian assistance, have established a presence in Port Sudan. This is particularly alarming given the strategic importance of Port Sudan as a hub for trade and military logistics. While the Sudanese Armed Forces (SAF) have historically received backing from various international actors, Iran's growing involvement signifies a shift in the balance of power. The Iranian Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC), known for its clandestine operations, is allegedly working to support Sudan's military in its ongoing conflicts, further complicating an already volatile situation.

The implications of this Iranian presence are manifold. Not only does it embolden the SAF in its struggle against the paramilitary Rapid Support Forces (RSF), but it also raises concerns among Gulf states regarding the security of their maritime routes. With Iran's track record of using proxy forces to expand its influence, the potential for increased unrest in the Red Sea region becomes a distinct possibility—one that Gulf states cannot afford to ignore.

Regional Reactions and Geopolitical Implications

The Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC) members, particularly Saudi Arabia and the United Arab Emirates, have reason to be apprehensive about Iran's encroachment into Sudan. Historically, these states have viewed Iran with suspicion, particularly in the context of its support for groups like the Houthis in Yemen and Hezbollah in Lebanon. The recent Iranian military activities in Port Sudan could be perceived as a direct challenge to their influence in the region.

Moreover, the visit of Sudan's General Abdel Fattah al-Burhan to Saudi Arabia, coupled with the simultaneous trip of Sudanese Prime Minister Abdalla Hamdok to Cairo, underscores the urgency with which regional powers are responding to the evolving situation. These visits signal a desire for closer ties with traditional allies, possibly as a counterbalance to Iranian influence. However, the effectiveness of these diplomatic overtures remains to be seen, particularly given the internal divisions within Sudan and the ongoing humanitarian crisis.

The Humanitarian Crisis: A Fertile Ground for Extremism

As the conflict drags on, Darfur remains a flashpoint for violence, displacement, and desperation. Reports indicate that thousands are fleeing their homes, facing starvation and rampant insecurity. The dire humanitarian situation is not only a tragedy in its own right but also a breeding ground for extremism and radicalization. The influx of arms and military support from foreign actors like Iran could further exacerbate these conditions, leading to a cycle of violence that is difficult to escape.

In this context, the role of international organizations and NGOs becomes crucial. However, with the increasing Iranian presence complicating the environment, delivering humanitarian assistance may become even more challenging. As aid agencies struggle to operate amidst violence, the potential for a complete breakdown of social order looms ever larger.

Looking Ahead: Navigating a Complex Landscape

The situation in Sudan is a reminder of the intricate web of local and international factors that influence stability in the Horn of Africa. The Iranian military involvement poses a significant challenge not just for Sudan but also for neighboring countries that rely on the stability of maritime routes. As regional powers like Saudi Arabia and the UAE seek to counterbalance Iranian influence, the effectiveness of their strategies will depend on their ability to engage with local dynamics and address the root causes of the conflict.

Moving forward, it is essential for international stakeholders to recognize that a military solution is not viable. Instead, a concerted effort to foster dialogue, support humanitarian efforts, and build local governance structures will be crucial in navigating this complex landscape. If these measures are not taken, the specter of Iranian influence in Sudan may herald a new era of instability that could reverberate throughout the Horn of Africa.
