The Unraveling of Sudan: Implications of Leadership Instability

OpinionSudanSouth Sudan By Amira Hassan  ·  July 7, 2026
Opinion notice: This is analysis and commentary by Horn Updates editors. It does not represent the position of any government, institution, or external party.
What this means
  • SPLM-IO faces leadership crisis
  • Abyei security talks at risk
  • Regional instability threatens peace efforts

Sudan is experiencing a critical juncture in its political history as instability among its leadership threatens both internal cohesion and regional partnerships. The recent resignation of prominent figures within the Sudan People’s Liberation Movement-In Opposition (SPLM-IO), including founding member Tijwog Agwet, underscores a growing dissatisfaction with the lack of democratic reforms and internal governance. This discontent is not just confined to the SPLM-IO; it reflects a broader malaise affecting the transitional government and poses serious questions about the future of governance in Sudan.

Leadership Crisis and Its Implications

The resignation of Agwet on October 15, 2023, signals a significant fracture within the SPLM-IO, a key player in the coalition that has governed South Sudan since the end of its civil war. Agwet's departure, citing failures in implementing promised democratic reforms, echoes a sentiment shared by many disillusioned citizens and political operatives. This discontent is compounded by the ongoing economic challenges facing the nation, including rampant inflation and widespread poverty, leading to a situation where public confidence in leadership is rapidly eroding.

As Agwet articulated in his resignation statement, the SPLM-IO has strayed from its original vision of fostering a democratic and inclusive political environment. His departure raises concerns about the party's stability and effectiveness at a time when cohesive leadership is essential for navigating both domestic challenges and relations with neighboring Sudan. The implications of this leadership crisis extend beyond party lines, potentially destabilizing the fragile peace that has characterized South Sudan since the peace agreement in 2018.

Abyei and Regional Security Concerns

Compounding these internal issues, the ongoing talks regarding the Abyei region—a contested area between Sudan and South Sudan—face renewed uncertainty. On October 14, 2023, the United Nations Interim Security Force for Abyei (UNISFA) chief concluded visits to both countries aimed at reinforcing security dialogue. However, the recent political upheaval within South Sudan raises questions about the government's ability to engage constructively in these discussions.

The Abyei region has long been a flashpoint for conflict, and any instability within the South Sudanese leadership could jeopardize ongoing peace efforts. If the SPLM-IO and other political factions cannot present a united front, the likelihood of escalated tensions with Sudan increases. The central government in Khartoum may seize upon internal discord as an opportunity to assert control over Abyei, further complicating the already tenuous peace in the region.

Economic Challenges Exacerbating Political Instability

The economic landscape in South Sudan is dire, with reports of government staff, including those from the Bank of South Sudan, staging sit-ins over salary arrears. These protests, motivated by the inability of the government to pay salaries, reflect a broader crisis of governance and economic mismanagement that continues to plague the nation. The resultant unrest not only destabilizes the political environment but also undermines public trust in the government’s ability to provide basic services.

The situation is exacerbated by the ongoing war in Sudan, which has resulted in a massive influx of refugees into South Sudan, further straining already limited resources. As the humanitarian situation in both countries deteriorates, the potential for civil unrest grows, creating a feedback loop that threatens to destabilize both Sudan and South Sudan. The economic and political fragility in South Sudan, coupled with the ongoing conflict in Sudan, creates a precarious situation where any spark could ignite widespread unrest.

Looking Ahead: The Path to Stability

As Sudan navigates this turbulent period, the international community’s role in fostering dialogue and stability becomes increasingly critical. Moving forward, it is essential for both Sudan and South Sudan to prioritize inclusive governance and financial accountability to rebuild public trust. The current crises provide an opportunity for both nations to reassess their leadership and governance structures, fostering an environment conducive to democratic reforms and stability.

However, this will require not just internal commitment but also sustained engagement from regional and international partners to facilitate dialogue and support economic recovery initiatives. As the situation stands, the future of both nations hangs in a delicate balance, reliant on their ability to forge a path toward cooperation and stability amidst the chaos. In this context, the coming months will be crucial in determining whether Sudan can stabilize its leadership and whether South Sudan can respond effectively to the challenges it faces.
