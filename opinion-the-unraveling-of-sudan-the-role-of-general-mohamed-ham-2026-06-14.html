The Unraveling of Sudan: The Role of General Mohamed Hamdan Dagalo in the Current Crisis

OpinionSudan By Amira Hassan  ·  June 14, 2026
Opinion notice: This is analysis and commentary by Horn Updates editors. It does not represent the position of any government, institution, or external party.
What this means
  • Sudan: Hemedti's power ambitions deepen conflict
  • Humanitarian crisis risks overwhelming aid efforts
  • International diplomatic efforts facing severe headwinds

The ongoing conflict in Sudan has escalated dramatically since April 2023, primarily driven by the power struggle between two military factions: the Sudanese Armed Forces (SAF) led by General Abdel Fattah al-Burhan and the Rapid Support Forces (RSF) commanded by General Mohamed Hamdan Dagalo, popularly known as Hemedti. As the fighting continues to ravage the country, Hemedti's actions and ambitions have emerged as a crucial factor not only in the humanitarian crisis but also in the broader geopolitical implications for the Horn of Africa.

Hemedti: The Face of a New Sudanese Power Dynamic

Hemedti's rise to power is emblematic of Sudan's tumultuous transition from dictatorship to a military-led governance structure following the ousting of Omar al-Bashir in 2019. Initially viewed as a potential partner in the transition to democracy, Hemedti's RSF has increasingly revealed itself as a key player in perpetuating instability. The RSF, composed largely of former Janjaweed militias implicated in the Darfur genocide, has used its significant military capabilities to assert control over strategic resources and territories, thereby complicating peace initiatives.

As of October 2023, the conflict has resulted in thousands of deaths, with millions displaced both internally and across borders. Hemedti's forces have been accused of committing gross human rights violations, including indiscriminate killings and looting, further alienating the civilian population. This has raised the stakes for international actors, who now face the daunting challenge of addressing a humanitarian emergency while navigating the treacherous waters of Sudanese politics.

Humanitarian Crisis: A Ticking Time Bomb

The humanitarian situation in Sudan has deteriorated to catastrophic levels. According to the United Nations, over 25 million people—about half the country's population—are in urgent need of assistance. Food security is at an all-time low, with the World Food Programme estimating that nearly 7 million people are on the brink of famine. The conflict has disrupted crucial supply chains, making it increasingly difficult for aid organizations to deliver essential services.

Hemedti's control over significant economic resources, including gold mines, has led to his increasing wealth and influence, which he has leveraged to maintain loyalty among his ranks. This financial power allows him to sustain military operations, exacerbating the humanitarian crisis further. As the conflict drags on, the international community must grapple with the reality that humanitarian aid is being weaponized, as both sides use food and resources as bargaining chips in their power struggle.

Regional and International Implications

The implications of Hemedti's actions extend far beyond Sudan's borders. Neighboring countries, including South Sudan and Chad, are already feeling the effects of the spillover from the conflict, with an influx of refugees straining their resources. Furthermore, the worsening humanitarian crisis poses a risk of destabilizing the entirety of the Horn of Africa, a region already fraught with its own challenges, including ethnic tensions and governance issues.

International diplomatic efforts are facing severe headwinds. The African Union and the Intergovernmental Authority on Development (IGAD) have struggled to mediate a ceasefire, as both military factions remain entrenched in their positions. Hemedti’s refusal to engage in meaningful dialogue is a significant barrier, further exacerbated by the lack of a unified international response. While countries like the United States have imposed sanctions on Hemedti and his associates, these measures have yet to yield tangible results.

Looking Ahead: Prospects for Stability

As the situation in Sudan continues to escalate, the urgent need for a comprehensive strategy to address both the humanitarian crisis and the political impasse becomes increasingly apparent. The international community must shift its focus from merely providing humanitarian aid to implementing a long-term strategy that addresses the root causes of the conflict. This includes engaging with all stakeholders, including civil society and marginalized groups, to forge a path toward inclusive governance.

Going forward, the future of Sudan hinges significantly on how General Hemedti navigates the complex landscape of power and influence. Should he continue down the path of militarization and human rights abuses, the country risks descending into a protracted conflict that could reverberate across the region. Alternatively, a shift toward negotiation and compromise could pave the way for a more stable and prosperous Sudan. The stakes are high, and the choices made in the coming months will be crucial in determining not just the fate of Sudan, but also the broader stability of the Horn of Africa.
