The Dual Challenge of Public Health and Governance in South Sudan

OpinionSouth Sudan By Amira Hassan  ·  May 4, 2026
Opinion notice: This is analysis and commentary by Horn Updates editors. It does not represent the position of any government, institution, or external party.
What this means
  • Unity State's health initiatives gaining traction
  • Governance issues continue to undermine stability
  • Need for comprehensive public health strategy

In recent weeks, South Sudan has witnessed a notable uptick in efforts to address public health issues and governance challenges, particularly with Unity State's ban on dangerous alcohol and drugs. This initiative, while commendable, underscores a dual challenge facing the young nation: the need for effective governance and a comprehensive public health strategy. As South Sudan continues to navigate the aftermath of prolonged conflict and instability, the implications of these initiatives extend far beyond the immediate public health concerns.

Unity State's Bold Move Against Alcohol and Drugs

On October 24, 2023, Unity State announced a ban on the production and sale of illicit alcohol and drugs, a move prompted by rising health crises linked to substance abuse. Local authorities reported that the consumption of dangerous substances has not only exacerbated health issues but also contributed to social unrest and violence. The governor of Unity State, Joseph Nguen Manytuil, stated, “This is a necessary step to protect our communities from the scourge of addiction and its associated crimes.”

Unity State’s initiative is particularly significant given the backdrop of South Sudan’s ongoing struggles with health care access and governance. The country has one of the world's highest rates of maternal and child mortality, exacerbated by poor health infrastructure and a lack of resources. The ban on dangerous substances serves as both a public health measure and a governance challenge, requiring local authorities to enforce the law effectively and educate communities about the dangers of substance abuse.

The Governance Challenge: A Fragile Framework

While Unity State's ban is a step in the right direction, it is essential to recognize the broader governance context in which these actions are taking place. The South Sudanese government has faced criticism for its inability to maintain law and order and effectively manage public resources. The recent detention of former finance official Majak Akech, whose family has reported ongoing uncertainty about his status, highlights the political tensions that continue to undermine governance structures.

Moreover, the ongoing economic struggles, particularly the dependency on oil revenues, have hampered the government’s ability to invest in sustainable health and social programs. As articulated in a recent opinion piece on economic diversification, South Sudan's heavy reliance on oil has stifled efforts to develop alternative sectors that could improve overall governance and public welfare. Without a comprehensive strategy to diversify the economy, initiatives like Unity State's ban may be thwarted by the very systemic issues they aim to resolve.

Public Health Infrastructure: A Critical Need

In tandem with the ban on dangerous substances, Western Equatoria State’s plan to build a high-tech laboratory for the detection of diseases such as Ebola and mpox is another significant move toward bolstering public health infrastructure. This initiative, announced on October 20, 2023, represents a recognition of the necessity for improved health facilities and disease prevention strategies in a region frequently threatened by outbreaks.

However, the success of such initiatives hinges on effective governance and the allocation of resources. The recent comments by Speaker of Parliament, Jemma Nunu Kumba, regarding the inadequacies of the pension system and the lack of accountability in salary deductions reflect a broader governance crisis that could undermine public health efforts. Without addressing these governance weaknesses, investing in health infrastructure may yield limited benefits.

Looking Ahead: Building a Sustainable Future

As South Sudan navigates this complex landscape of public health and governance, the coming months will be crucial. The dual challenges of enforcing bans on harmful substances and developing robust health infrastructure must be approached with a coordinated strategy that includes community engagement, transparent governance, and economic diversification. The United Nations and other international partners can play a vital role in supporting these efforts by providing technical assistance and facilitating dialogue among stakeholders.

To foster a sustainable future, South Sudan must not only focus on immediate public health policies but also address the underlying governance issues that have historically impeded progress. A commitment to transparent and accountable governance, alongside robust public health initiatives, could pave the way for a healthier and more stable South Sudan.
