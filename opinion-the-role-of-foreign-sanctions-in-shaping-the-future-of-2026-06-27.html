The Role of Foreign Sanctions in Shaping the Future of Sudan’s Conflict

OpinionSudan By Amira Hassan  ·  June 27, 2026
Opinion notice: This is analysis and commentary by Horn Updates editors. It does not represent the position of any government, institution, or external party.
What this means
  • U.S. sanctions targeting key actors intensifying
  • Humanitarian crisis worsening amid conflict
  • Regional stability increasingly at risk

As Sudan continues to grapple with the devastating consequences of the ongoing conflict, the recent imposition of U.S. sanctions has emerged as a significant factor that could reshape the landscape of the war. On October 26, 2023, the U.S. Treasury Department sanctioned several individuals and firms linked to the ongoing violence, emphasizing the need to hold accountable those fueling the fighting. However, there is a vital question that arises: will these sanctions bring about meaningful change, or will they further entrench the power dynamics that have long plagued Sudan?

The Nature of the Sanctions

The targeted sanctions are a response to the escalating humanitarian crisis resulting from the conflict between the Sudan Armed Forces (SAF) and the Rapid Support Forces (RSF), both of which have been implicated in widespread human rights abuses. The U.S. sanctions specifically focus on individuals and companies that are believed to be providing financial and logistical support to the warring factions. Such actions are not new; sanctions have been a tool of U.S. foreign policy in Sudan for decades, aimed at curbing the influence of oppressive regimes. However, the effectiveness of these measures has often been questionable.

In previous instances, sanctions have had mixed results, sometimes failing to achieve their intended outcomes while inadvertently harming the civilian population. The recent sanctions come on the heels of a dire humanitarian situation, with millions displaced and in urgent need of assistance. According to the United Nations, more than 27 million people—over half of Sudan's population—are in need of humanitarian aid, a stark reality that is likely to escalate as the conflict endures.

The Impact on Military Dynamics

The sanctions may indeed have an impact on the financial networks that support the SAF and RSF. However, both factions have demonstrated a remarkable capacity to adapt and sustain their operations despite international pressure. The RSF, for instance, has leveraged its control over lucrative gold mines to fund its activities, while the SAF has maintained access to state resources and foreign military support. As such, the sanctions may not significantly disrupt the flow of resources to these military groups, potentially allowing them to continue their brutal campaigns against one another and against civilians.

Moreover, the conflict's entrenched nature suggests that sanctions alone will not be sufficient to alter the power dynamics at play. The leaders of both the SAF and RSF, General Abdel Fattah al-Burhan and General Mohamed Hamdan Dagalo, respectively, have demonstrated their willingness to engage in prolonged warfare to achieve their objectives. The absence of a coherent and unified opposition further complicates the situation, as various armed groups vie for power and influence, often aligning with foreign interests that may not be aligned with the U.S. sanctions strategy.

The Broader Regional Implications

The ramifications of Sudan's conflict extend well beyond its borders, posing a significant risk to regional stability. Neighboring countries such as South Sudan and Chad have already been affected by the influx of refugees and the potential for cross-border violence. The U.S. sanctions could inadvertently exacerbate these issues by pushing the warring factions to seek support from non-state actors or external powers, potentially leading to a wider regional conflict.

Furthermore, the sanctions may complicate diplomatic efforts aimed at resolving the crisis. Countries in the region, including Egypt and Saudi Arabia, have vested interests in Sudan and may not align with U.S. policies. Their attempts to mediate the conflict could be hampered by the sanctions, which may be perceived as an affront to their sovereignty or as a limitation on their ability to influence the situation positively.

Looking Ahead: The Need for a Comprehensive Approach

While the U.S. sanctions represent a crucial step in holding those responsible for the violence accountable, they must be part of a broader strategy that includes diplomatic engagement and humanitarian support. A successful resolution to the conflict will require not only pressuring the military leaders but also addressing the underlying grievances that have fueled the violence, such as economic disparities, ethnic tensions, and lack of political representation.

Moving forward, the international community must work towards a multifaceted approach that combines sanctions with proactive diplomacy and significant humanitarian assistance. Engaging regional players in a constructive dialogue will be critical in fostering a consensus on how to stabilize Sudan and address the root causes of the ongoing conflict. The stakes are high, not just for Sudan but for the stability of the Horn of Africa as a whole. The coming months will be pivotal in determining whether the latest sanctions will contribute to a meaningful shift in the conflict or merely serve as another layer in a complex web of violence and instability.
