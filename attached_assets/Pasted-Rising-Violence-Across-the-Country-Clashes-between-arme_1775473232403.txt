Rising Violence Across the Country

Clashes between armed groups and government-aligned forces have increased in recent months, particularly in Upper Nile, Jonglei, and Unity states.

But the violence is no longer only political.

Local conflicts — often driven by cattle raiding, land disputes, and access to resources like gold — are intensifying and becoming deadlier. These localized clashes are feeding into broader national instability.

👉 What this means:
Violence is spreading from isolated incidents into a wider pattern that is harder to control.

🗳️ Elections Are Coming — But the System Isn’t Ready

South Sudan is expected to hold elections in 2026. On paper, this should mark a transition toward stability.

In reality, key conditions are missing:

Weak institutions
Limited trust between political rivals
Incomplete peace agreement implementation

👉 What this means:
Elections could become a trigger for conflict rather than a solution.

🏃 A Growing Displacement Crisis

Hundreds of thousands of people have been forced from their homes due to violence and insecurity.

Many are living in:

temporary shelters
flood-affected areas
regions with little access to aid

👉 What this means:
Large-scale displacement increases pressure on already fragile communities and fuels further conflict.

🍞 Food Insecurity Is Reaching Critical Levels

A significant portion of the population is facing severe hunger.

Conflict, flooding, and economic disruption are limiting:

food production
supply chains
humanitarian access

👉 What this means:
Hunger is not just a humanitarian issue — it is a driver of instability and conflict.

🌊 Climate Pressure Is Making Everything Worse

Recurring floods have destroyed homes, displaced communities, and reduced access to farmland.

This has intensified competition over:

land
water
grazing areas

👉 What this means:
Environmental stress is accelerating existing tensions.

🧠 The Bigger Picture: A “Polycrisis”

South Sudan is not facing a single issue — it is facing multiple crises at once:

Political instability
Armed conflict
Economic hardship
Food insecurity
Climate shocks

Each of these problems feeds into the others.

👉 This is what makes the current moment especially dangerous.

⚠️ What Comes Next

The next 6–12 months will be critical.

If violence continues to rise and political tensions escalate ahead of elections, South Sudan could move closer to renewed large-scale conflict.

But if stability efforts hold — including political dialogue and humanitarian support — the country may still avoid that outcome.
